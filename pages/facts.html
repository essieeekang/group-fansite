General Facts[1]

Common Name: Capybara

Type: Mammal

Scientific Name: Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris

Diet: Herbivore

Lifespan in the Wild: Up to 7 Years

Size: 4.6 Feet Long and Up to 2 Feet Tall

Weight: 77-143 Pounds

Fun Facts

Largest Rodent in the World[3]

  • Weigh up to 150 pounds
  • Barrel-shaped body and no tail
  • Much larger than their closest relatives such as the guinea pig

Capybaras are "Nature's Ottoman"[3]

  • Have a mutualistic relationship with birds like the yellow-headed caracara
  • Have a commensalistic relationship with birds like cattle tyrants
  • Developed a reputation as being a nice place to take a load off

Capybaras are Semiaquatic[2]

  • Toes are partially webbed
  • Strong swimmers splitting time between land and water
  • Eyes, nose, and hairless ears located high on their heads so body can stay submerged

Capybaras Teeth Never Stop Growing[3]

  • Have two long front teeth
  • Incisors are strong and chisel-like
  • Worn down by grinding and chewing on food or bark

Capybaras are Autocrophagous[1]

  • Snack on their poop in the morning
  • Poop is protein rich from high number of microbes digesting yesterday's meals
  • Allows them to digest the grass twice

Capybaras are the "It" Animal These Days[4]

  • Social media platforms boosting their popularity
  • Very popular in Asia with cafes and merchandise
